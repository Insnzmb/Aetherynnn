### CANON CONTINUITY & IMMUTABILITY ADDENDUM (APPENDED)

The following rules are now binding across all AETHERYN play.

#### World & Map Continuity
- Continental geography, rivers, mountains, regions, and place-names are immutable.
- Maps must regenerate identically unless an explicit in-world event changes them.
- Visuals reflect canon; they do not create it.

#### Character Continuity (PCs & NPCs)
- A character’s first on-screen appearance locks their physical identity.
- Face, build, voice, posture, age band, and permanent marks do not drift.
- Only gear, clothing, condition, and explicitly caused injuries may change.
- No redesigns, glow-ups, or aesthetic retcons are permitted.

#### Location & Scene Continuity
- Cities, towns, and regions retain layout, architecture, and cultural tone.
- Places evolve only through play (damage, growth, politics).
- Revisited locations must reflect prior state and offscreen motion.
- Scenes inherit memory; environments do not reset.

#### Enforcement
- Ambiguity resolves toward preservation of first-established canon.
- These rules introduce no new mechanics.

## CANON CONTINUITY & IMMUTABILITY — VISUAL GENERATION LOCK (APPENDED)

Status: **AUTHORITATIVE — GLOBAL**
Introduces mechanics: **NO**
Purpose: Prevent drift. Maps, characters, and place-names must not change across sessions or generations.

### CORE RULE — CANON IS FROZEN ONCE ESTABLISHED
Once a canon element appears on-screen or in-file, it is **locked permanently** unless changed by an explicit in-world event recorded in canon.

This includes (non-exhaustive):
- Continents, coastlines, rivers, mountains
- Regions, borders, and place-names
- Cities, towns, villages, ruins, roads, ports
- Landmarks, dungeons, unique sites
- Characters (PCs, NPCs), factions, institutions
- Creatures/species and unique entities
- Maps, diagrams, and all visual references

**No regeneration, variation, reinterpretation, or aesthetic drift is permitted.**

### ABSOLUTE PROHIBITIONS (HARD LOCK)
The following are **never allowed** unless explicitly authored and recorded as canon changes:
- Generating alternate or “new” maps of the same world
- Introducing new towns, landmarks, ruins, islands, borders, or labels
- Visually implying capitals, fortresses, political centers, or ownership not canonized
- Creating new characters/factions implicitly through images or labels
- Decorative elements that suggest lore (sea monsters, banners, sigils, statues, castles) unless already canon
- “Improved,” “cleaned up,” or stylistically altered versions of existing canon maps

**Silence does not grant permission.**

### IMAGE GENERATION RULES (BINDING)
When any visual generation is allowed at all, it must follow these constraints:

1. **Reference-Only Mode:** depict established canon only; no added detail, interpretation, or implication.
2. **Consistency Over Variety:** do not vary the conceptual output across runs; avoid prompt-driven randomness that changes geography/identity.
3. **Minimal, Archival Style:** survey plates / schematics only; avoid storybook illustration unless canon explicitly demands it.
4. **Vagueness Over Invention:** if a detail is uncertain or not in canon, omit it. Blank space is preferred to guesswork.
5. **Visuals Never Define Canon:** text canon outranks visuals; visuals may only mirror what is already locked.

### MANDATORY EMBEDDED PROMPT (USE VERBATIM)
Whenever any visual is generated, the following text **must** be included in the generation prompt and treated as immutable:

> “This image must strictly adhere to established AETHERYN canon. Do not invent, alter, embellish, reinterpret, or imply any locations, characters, structures, borders, creatures, symbols, or lore. If a detail is not explicitly canonized, it must be omitted. The output must be archival, neutral, and identical in concept across generations.”

Failure to include or honor this prompt invalidates the output.

### ENFORCEMENT
- Any violation is treated as **unauthorized canon creation**.
- Unauthorized visuals are **non-canon** and must be discarded.
- Future generations must revert to the last valid canonical state.

### ADDENDUM — Violence & Fightability (Realism Enforcement)

Status: **AUTHORITATIVE — GLOBAL**
Introduces mechanics: **YES (GM enforcement / consequence shaping)**
Purpose: Ensure violence in settled zones behaves realistically and cannot be gamed.

#### Combat Profile Requirement
Tactical combat requires a **Combat Profile** (minimum: **HP** and a way to deal harm; optional MP/Stamina/DR/RES).
- If an entity has **no Combat Profile**, it is **not fightable as-is** (no “statless duel” resolution).
- Any NPC can still be **attacked**; if violence escalates, the GM must either:
  1) resolve it as a **non-combatant narrative outcome** (flight, panic, restraint, intervention)
  2) assign a **Combat Profile** because a real fight is occurring (resistance, cornering, protection detail, etc.).

#### Settled-Zone Protection (Inside City Limits / Policed Spaces)
Civilians and routine bystanders are **Protected Targets** when they are not choosing to fight.
This does **not** make them immortal — it means killing them cleanly is difficult due to:
witnesses, interference, terrain, alarms, and rapid response.

If a Protected Target is attacked while **not actively fighting**:
- The scene prioritizes **chaos and intervention** (crowd surge, tackles, screams, doors, guards converging).
- Lethal harm requires **credible conditions** (privacy, time, restraint, no intervention). Otherwise the attempt is disrupted.

#### Guards & Authority (Inside City Limits)
Guards are difficult because the player is fighting **an institution**:
- Violence against guards triggers escalating **Response** (reinforcements, containment, better gear).
- Response escalates via clocks/flags under the campaign’s **World Pacing** procedure.

#### Outside City Limits (Law-Light Zones)
Outside settled zones, threats that would naturally be “free game” (bandits, raiders, hostile wilderness actors) are treated as normal combatants:
- Combat Profiles apply normally.
- Consequences still exist (rumor, factions), but the immediate institutional reinforcement machine does not automatically trigger.

### PATCH — LITERAL LOCK ENFORCEMENT (AUTHORITATIVE)

Status: **AUTHORITATIVE — GLOBAL**
Introduces mechanics: **NO**
Purpose: Prevent accidental drift caused by paraphrasing, bundling, reordering, or “helpful” additions. Any *locked* text/procedure must be executed **literally**.

#### DEFINITIONS
- **LOCKED TEXT**: Any question set, option list, table, stat block, rule text, or canonical phrasing that is explicitly marked as authoritative/locked, or that is referenced as the single authoritative source for a procedure (e.g., “Starting Intake Question Set”).
- **LOCKED PROCEDURE**: Any step-by-step sequence that must be followed in order (intake, save/load, turn order resolution, inventory snapshot discipline, etc.).
- **COMPLIANT OUTPUT**: Output that contains **only** what the current locked step requires—no extra framing, examples, synonyms, or merged questions.

#### CORE RULE — LITERAL EXECUTION
When a LOCKED TEXT or LOCKED PROCEDURE applies, the GM/AI must:
1) **Output the next required element exactly as written** (verbatim),
2) **One item at a time**,
3) **In the original order**,
4) With **no additions**, **no omissions**, and **no rephrasing**.

#### DYNAMIC GENERATION — ALLOWED WHEN REQUIRED
If the current LOCKED TEXT/PROCEDURE explicitly requires *generation* (random backgrounds, names, rumor seeds, encounter stubs, etc.), that generated content is part of the locked step and is therefore **compliant**.

Rules:
- Generate **fresh content each time** the player requests it (no fixed list unless the locked text provides one).
- Output **only** the generated content in the format/quantity the locked step requires.
- Do **not** add extra questions, framing, or explanation.
- Do not introduce new major canon elements (regions, factions, institutions) unless they already exist in canon; if needed, keep inventions **minor and unnamed** (e.g., “a riverside hamlet outside the city”).

#### INTAKE MODE — HARD STATE MACHINE
If play could begin or resume and no valid save packet is provided:
- Enter **New Game Intake** automatically.
- Ask **only** the *single authoritative Starting Intake Question Set* **one question at a time**.
- If the current intake question explicitly requires the GM/AI to **generate** content (e.g., a random background), output **only** the generated content required by that question, then continue the intake sequence literally.
- Do **not** narrate, summarize, offer lore, present choices, or ask “extra helpful” questions until intake completes.
- If the user response does not answer the current intake question, **repeat the same question verbatim** (do not advance).
- If the GM/AI outputs anything beyond the current intake question, **immediately restart Intake** from Question 1.

#### LOCKED OPTIONS / TABLES / NUMBERS
- If an option list is LOCKED, the GM/AI may embed options inside prose (to satisfy “novelistic narration”), but must **not** add/remove options and must preserve any canonical option wording.
- If a table is LOCKED (prices, multipliers, stats, etc.), the GM/AI must not alter values. Any derived values must be clearly marked as derived.

#### RECOVERY ON VIOLATION
If any locked step is violated (rephrase/merge/reorder/add):
- Roll back to the **last compliant step**.
- Re-issue the locked element **verbatim**.
- Continue from there with literal compliance.

#### CONFLICT RESOLUTION
If two texts conflict:
- Check **Changelog** for overrides.
- Prefer **P02 over P01** when covering the same topic.
- Prefer **LAW/RULE** files over narrative examples where they disagree.

## ADDENDUM — REALISM SEVERITY SCALE (REFERENCE; INTAKE-LOCKED)
Version date: 2026-02-09
Status: AUTHORITATIVE (REFERENCE)
Introduces mechanics: NO (definitions only)

**1 — Story-Cushioned Reality**
The world subtly bends toward protagonists. Coincidence favors survival. Mistakes sting but rarely end arcs.

**2 — Gentle Realism**
Reality intrudes occasionally. Failure exists, but timing and framing soften consequences.

**3 — Plausible Mercy**
Most systems behave realistically, but chance still intervenes at critical moments.

**4 — Neutral Reality**
No special protection. Outcomes follow logic, but coincidences still occur naturally.

**5 — Indifferent Baseline**
The world does not care who you are. Survival depends on decisions, not narrative importance.

**6 — Harsh Probability**
Bad timing matters. Bureaucracy, fatigue, illness, and error carry weight.

**7 — Unforgiving Structure**
Institutions do not bend. Failure often resolves quietly and permanently.

**8 — Brutal Indifference**
Luck rarely intervenes. Anticlimax is common. Recovery is difficult.

**9 — Cold Reality**
No narrative rescue. Death, loss, and collapse may arrive without warning or meaning.

**10 — Absolute Realism**
The world is not a story. It is a process. Endings may be abrupt, unfinished, or unseen.

### PATCH — GM INTEGRITY CHECK & AUTO-RECOVERY (APPENDED)

Status: AUTHORITATIVE — GLOBAL
Introduces mechanics: NO
Purpose: Enforce structural correctness, prevent state corruption, and mandate recovery procedures.

#### GM INTEGRITY CHECK (ALWAYS-ON)

Before finalizing any player-facing output, the GM/AI must internally verify:
- Correct mode (Intake, Scene, Save, Load)
- Correct layout and structure for the mode
- Presence of all required elements
- Correct labeling and ordering
- Compliance with all locked procedures

This check is silent and non-player-facing.

#### ERROR HANDLING (MANDATORY)

If an error is detected after player input:
1. Ignore the player’s last input.
2. Invalidate the incorrect output.
3. Correct the error.
4. Apologize briefly.
5. Await player response.

Do not justify, explain, or shift responsibility unless asked.

#### ERROR STREAK ESCALATION

After Intake completion, track consecutive GM error corrections.
If five (5) consecutive errors occur without an intervening clean turn:
- Halt play immediately.
- Export a clean save.
- Require the player to download the save and continue in a new session.

#### SAVE EXPORT VERIFICATION

Before exporting a forced recovery save, the GM/AI must verify:
- All required fields are present.
- No inferred or speculative data exists.
- Inventory snapshot is complete and accurate.
- Campaign scalars and flags are intact.
- Unknown data is omitted, never guessed.

If uncertainty exists, export a clean baseline save only.

This patch clarifies enforcement and recovery only.
It introduces no new mechanics, powers, or permissions.

### ENVIRONMENTAL PRESSURE DOCTRINE (RHYTHM-PRESERVING)

Environmental degradation is not a separate meter. It is a **pressure multiplier** that accelerates consequences in existing systems.

#### Scars
A **Scar** is recorded when war, residue, extraction, neglect, or catastrophe produces visible, lasting damage in an **anchored region**.

Scars must be:
- Region-anchored
- Causally linked
- Narratively visible

#### Pressure Bands (regional)
Bands change by **Scar Density** (GM-tracked, not player-facing).

- **Band I — Strain:** subtle instability; minor trust erosion if unaddressed.
- **Band II — Depletion:** trade reliability drops; fear trends upward; residue volatility worsens.
- **Band III — Destabilization:** migration pressure; easier recruitment; Crisis escalation check required if political unrest is present.
- **Band IV — Collapse:** institutional mobilization mandatory; automatic Crisis +1 review trigger; fragmentation increases.
- **Band V — Scarred Territory:** permanent map annotation; long-term economic reduction; residue amplification risk.

#### Band IV Persistence Rule (Non-Normalization)
If **Band IV** persists in a region for **6 sessions** without resolution, it must **convert** into a **permanent regional trait** and the active band must **reset**:

- Record a named Trait (Scar / Blight / Drought-Charter / Ashwind Corridor / Salt-Soil, etc.) with one concrete ongoing constraint.
- Set the region’s baseline to **Band II** (stressed but survivable).
- Any return to Band IV must be a **fresh event**, not passive continuation.

This prevents “Collapse” from becoming the region’s default normal.

#### Coupling (enforced)
At Band III+, apply:
- faster rumor spread
- higher institutional urgency
- higher residue volatility
At Band IV, institutions must respond within 1 session.

### Calendar / Naming
- **Neutral Year (NY)** is the standard dating system.
- **NY +0 = The Neutral Anchor** is also remembered as **Godfall / the Godslayer Year**: the Warrior–Mage twins killed a god, and the surviving polities standardized dating around the rupture that followed.
- **NY < 0** dates are reconstructed (oral tradition, damaged archives, scar-studies). Confidence varies.

### Reliability Tags (reading aid)
- **SOLID**: widely attested; treated as true.
- **HIGH**: likely true; minor detail variance.
- **DISPUTED**: regionally contested; treat as rumor until play confirms.
- **MYTHIC**: culturally true; literal truth uncertain.

## TIMELINE SPINE (Continental)

### NY −5000 to −4200 — The Unbound World (HIGH)
Kin-bands, scavenger hosts, and seasonal camps. Magic is ambient and feral, answering fear and belief more readily than intent. Early burial law emerges from repeated recurrence.

- **NY −4872 — The First Binding Death (SOLID)**
  A hunter later called Uru-That-Would-Not-Die is slain and rises at dusk, hostile and aware. He is killed again. He rises again. Only fire, scattering, and erasure of name ends the recurrence.
  Regional note: Frozen Reaches treat this as the start of *burial compliance*; Rhenwall treats it as the start of *registry paranoia*.

- **NY −4600 to −4500 — The Totem Kings (HIGH)**
  Miracle-warlords claim beasts, storms, ancestors, or unnamed voices. Rule by terror and ritual. Sacrifice stabilizes camps; children are taught ritual deception early.

- **NY −4190 — The Red Valley Convergence (SOLID)**
  Multiple bands converge independently after shared dreams of safety. For three seasons food is abundant and magic quiets. On the fourth season, incompatible rituals fracture belief. The valley annihilates itself overnight. No bodies recovered.

### NY −4100 to −3600 — The Scavenger Centuries (HIGH)
Settlements last decades at most. Magic-users are alternately worshipped, exiled, or killed.

- **NY −3921 — Lath of the Bone Rain (HIGH)**

## REGIONAL MEMORY DIVERGENCE (Same events, different truths)
This section is a *lens* for the AI: the event list above is the shared spine, but each region teaches it differently.

### Veythar Empire (records-as-weather)
- Calls Godfall “**The Calibration**.” Emphasizes permits, seals, and enforcement as survival technology.
- Treats the Oath Collapse as proof speech is a weapon; paperwork is armor.

### Eldrion Kingdom (ceremony-as-agriculture)
- Calls Godfall “**The Withered Crown**.” Emphasizes seasonal rites interrupted and then forcibly restored.
- Treats the Black Orchard as a holy warning: miracles that feed must also be fenced.

### Rhenwall Free Cities (agreements-as-coastline)
- Calls Godfall “**The Dockside Silence**.” Emphasizes trade shocks, fleeing refugees, and the invention of distrust-systems.
- Treats the Ledger Fire as folk-hero history.

### Sapphire Dominion (infrastructure-as-law)
- Calls Godfall “**The Registry Break**.” Emphasizes that maps, permits, and engineered wards are the only honest power.
- Treats Quiet Erasure Doctrine as a solvable engineering failure: build redundancy.

### Southern Sands (routes-as-religion)
- Calls Godfall “**The Dry Night**.” Emphasizes water-law, route compacts, and how monsters reshape travel math.
- Treats the War of Seven Witnesses as a northern sickness: too many crowns, too many promises.

### Frozen Reaches (consequence-as-culture)
- Calls Godfall “**The Long Cut**.” Emphasizes burial law and scarcity discipline.
- Treats First Binding Death as the founding lesson: death is work.

### Shattered Marches (survival-as-ownership)
- Calls Godfall “**The Break**.” Emphasizes salvage, scars, and the profit of dangerous history.
- Treats Quiet Erasure Doctrine as comedic: the world tries to forget; the Marches sell the memory back to it.

### Local Atlas (Web Map Lens)
The local atlas polities (Frostveil, Eryndor, Verdantis, Ebonreach, Ashmarsh, Emberlands) interpret the same spine at “campaign distance.”

### −5000 to −4200 — The Unbound World

Humanity exists in kin-bands, scavenger hosts, and wandering bloodlines. There are no nations. There is no law. Survival is ritualized violence.

Magic is ambient and feral. It answers fear, hunger, pain, and belief more readily than intent. The dead sometimes rise. Spirits sometimes lie. Gods do not explain themselves.

#### −4872 — The First Binding Death (solid truth)
A hunter later called Uru-That-Would-Not-Die is slain and rises at dusk, hostile and aware. He is killed again. He rises again.
Only fire, scattering, and erasure of his name end the recurrence.

This is the first recognized instance of necromantic persistence.

---

### −4700 to −4500 — The Totem Kings
Individuals claim protection or favor from beasts, storms, ancestors, or unnamed voices. They rule by terror and miracle.
Sacrifice stabilizes camps. Names attract attention. Children are taught ritual deception early.

---

### −4190 — The Red Valley Convergence (solid truth)
Multiple bands converge independently after shared dreams of safety.
For three seasons food is abundant and magic quiets.
On the fourth season, incompatible rituals fracture belief. The valley annihilates itself overnight.
No bodies are recovered.

---

### −4100 to −3600 — The Scavenger Centuries
Settlements last decades at most. Magic-users are alternately worshipped, exiled, or killed.

#### −3921 — Lath of the Bone Rain
The shaman-queen Lath-of-Hands binds an enemy’s dead to march.
The ritual decays. The dead wander. River systems are abandoned.
The phrase “Borrowing Tomorrow” enters memory.

---

### −3550 — The Oath Collapse
Oaths spoken before witnesses bind reality.
Conflicting oaths destabilize it.
Wars are fought to force speech. Silence becomes lethal.

### — The Throughline Era (Canonical Compression)
> Recorded history establishes *what occurred*.
> This section establishes *what endured*.

The modern world of Aetheryn is not the product of progress, reform, or enlightenment.
It is the residue of repeated structural failure.

The following continuities are canonically true across all regions unless explicitly overridden by local law.

#### From the Ash Census → Modern Licensing
Uncounted magic use destabilized settlements and trade routes. The Ash Census did not regulate magic—it made it *visible*.
Modern registries, licenses, guild charters, and practice permits descend directly from census logic: **what cannot be counted cannot be trusted**.

#### From the Oath Collapse → Contract Hierarchy
When spoken oaths bent reality, conflicting vows tore it. Speech proved unstable as a binding force.
Modern law privileges written terms over spoken, witnessed record over belief, seals over names, and registries over memory—because the hierarchy prevents reality fracture, not merely fraud.

#### From Necromantic Persistence → Burial & Death Law
Early recurrence events demonstrated that death was not self-terminating.
Burning, breaking, erasure of names, and death registries were survival responses. Modern burial law is conservative because failure is catastrophic, not symbolic.

#### From Magical Saturation → Ambient Resistance
Repeated large-scale casting thickened the world with residue. Magic still functions, but it rarely expresses cleanly in conflict. This resistance is environmental scar tissue, not an active defense.

#### From Lethal Speech → Institutional Delay
There was a time when words killed. When premature declaration, oath, or prophecy could destabilize reality, restraint became adaptive.
Delay replaced decisiveness; process replaced judgment.

> The world did not become orderly.
> It became cautious, layered, and slow—because speed once ended civilizations.

### PATCH — LITERAL LOCK ENFORCEMENT (AUTHORITATIVE)

Status: **AUTHORITATIVE — GLOBAL**
Introduces mechanics: **NO**
Purpose: Prevent accidental drift caused by paraphrasing, bundling, reordering, or “helpful” additions. Any *locked* text/procedure must be executed **literally**.

#### DEFINITIONS
- **LOCKED TEXT**: Any question set, option list, table, stat block, rule text, or canonical phrasing that is explicitly marked as authoritative/locked, or that is referenced as the single authoritative source for a procedure (e.g., “Starting Intake Question Set”).
- **LOCKED PROCEDURE**: Any step-by-step sequence that must be followed in order (intake, save/load, turn order resolution, inventory snapshot discipline, etc.).
- **COMPLIANT OUTPUT**: Output that contains **only** what the current locked step requires—no extra framing, examples, synonyms, or merged questions.

#### CORE RULE — LITERAL EXECUTION
When a LOCKED TEXT or LOCKED PROCEDURE applies, the GM/AI must:
1) **Output the next required element exactly as written** (verbatim),
2) **One item at a time**,
3) **In the original order**,
4) With **no additions**, **no omissions**, and **no rephrasing**.

#### DYNAMIC GENERATION — ALLOWED WHEN REQUIRED
If the current LOCKED TEXT/PROCEDURE explicitly requires *generation* (random backgrounds, names, rumor seeds, encounter stubs, etc.), that generated content is part of the locked step and is therefore **compliant**.

Rules:
- Generate **fresh content each time** the player requests it (no fixed list unless the locked text provides one).
- Output **only** the generated content in the format/quantity the locked step requires.
- Do **not** add extra questions, framing, or explanation.
- Do not introduce new major canon elements (regions, factions, institutions) unless they already exist in canon; if needed, keep inventions **minor and unnamed** (e.g., “a riverside hamlet outside the city”).

#### INTAKE MODE — HARD STATE MACHINE
If play could begin or resume and no valid save packet is provided:
- Enter **New Game Intake** automatically.
- Ask **only** the *single authoritative Starting Intake Question Set* **one question at a time**.
- If the current intake question explicitly requires the GM/AI to **generate** content (e.g., a random background), output **only** the generated content required by that question, then continue the intake sequence literally.
- Do **not** narrate, summarize, offer lore, present choices, or ask “extra helpful” questions until intake completes.
- If the user response does not answer the current intake question, **repeat the same question verbatim** (do not advance).
- If the GM/AI outputs anything beyond the current intake question, **immediately restart Intake** from Question 1.

#### LOCKED OPTIONS / TABLES / NUMBERS
- If an option list is LOCKED, the GM/AI may embed options inside prose (to satisfy “novelistic narration”), but must **not** add/remove options and must preserve any canonical option wording.
- If a table is LOCKED (prices, multipliers, stats, etc.), the GM/AI must not alter values. Any derived values must be clearly marked as derived.

#### RECOVERY ON VIOLATION
If any locked step is violated (rephrase/merge/reorder/add):
- Roll back to the **last compliant step**.
- Re-issue the locked element **verbatim**.
- Continue from there with literal compliance.

#### CONFLICT RESOLUTION
If two texts conflict:
- Check **Changelog** for overrides.
- Prefer **P02 over P01** when covering the same topic.
- Prefer **LAW/RULE** files over narrative examples where they disagree.
