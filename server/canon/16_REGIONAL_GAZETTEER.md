### ERA DESIGNATION
The Hardened Quiet

A period defined not by open war, but by accumulated memory:
- Institutions track residue.
- Markets react to instability.
- Magic is monitored, not worshipped.
- Rumor spreads faster than armies.

- Expanding institutional surveillance of magic use.
- Border tensions stable but watchful.
- Shock-ward technologies increasingly standardized.

- Internal court politics more volatile than external threats.
- Agricultural stability holding.
- Sun-court rituals publicly reinforced.

- Trade dominance rising.

- Bureaucratic consolidation ongoing.

## REGION PROFILES (Macro-Regions)

### Veythar Empire
Veythar runs on record-keeping as a form of weather control. The empire believes disorder is a kind of contagious residue: invisible until it isn’t.

Governance: layered offices, overlapping mandates, and “temporary” emergency authorities that never quite expire.
Magic posture: permissive only when logged; suspicion is procedural, not personal.
Everyday texture: ink-stained fingertips, stamped travel slips, watchmen with ledger satchels.
Trade: iron tools, shock-ward fittings, paper, lamp oil.
Common taboo: unregistered miracles in public spaces.

Speech + naming: clipped official phrasing; surnames often occupational or place-tied (“of Ironspire,” “Ward-Drake,” “Sealwright”).

### Eldrion Kingdom
Eldrion is polite soil and weaponized ceremony. The Sun-Court frames stability as sacred agriculture: a harvest is proof the kingdom is still loved.

Governance: noble houses orbiting ritual legitimacy; public festivals conceal private leverage.
Magic posture: elemental craft is respected when bound to seasonal rites; Soul work is treated as a contaminant.
Everyday texture: orchard walls, sun-marked lintels, dusk bells, communal bread ovens.
Trade: fruit spirits, honeyed grain, fine cloth, ritual wax.

Speech + naming: lyrical honorifics; given names favor open vowels; places often end in -vale, -keep, -field.

### Rhenwall Free Cities
Rhenwall is a coastline of agreements. Nobody trusts anybody, so everyone builds systems that run on distrust.

Governance: city-states; councils; dockside arbitration enforced by access, not armies.
Magic posture: tolerated if it makes ships safer; prosecuted if it makes debts disappear.
Everyday texture: salt on everything, tariff chalk marks, dock cranes, galvanic warning lamps.
Trade: ship parts, rope, cured fish, copper wire, tariffs as political tools.

Speech + naming: blunt, transactional. Names skew practical; places often include water-terms (“Highwater,” “Port,” “Reach,” “Delta”).

### Sapphire Dominion
Sapphire is paperwork with teeth. It does not threaten loudly; it simply outlasts.

Governance: registries, permits, and infrastructure as statecraft.
Magic posture: “licensed craft” is fine; “unfiled craft” is treated as sabotage.
Everyday texture: queue lines, seal stamps, permit boards, fixed-hour gate inspections.
Trade: stone, glass, engineered wards, bureaucratic services.

Speech + naming: formal, layered titles; surnames often derived from archival lineages. Place names favor “Gate,” “Archive,” “Crossroads,” “Ward.”

### Southern Sands
The Southern Sands are a logistics miracle held together by stubborn people and better water math.

Governance: route compacts and caravan guilds; oaths matter because maps fail.
Everyday texture: wind scarves, sun-scarred leather, night travel, water-law etiquette (who drinks first is policy).
Trade: salt, minerals, glass, spices, salvage.

Speech + naming: short names, strong consonants; places often reference crossings, wells, and dunes.

### Frozen Reaches
The Frozen Reaches run on consequence, not comfort. The land is beautiful in the way a blade is beautiful.

Governance: wardens and survival councils; law is mostly burial law and ration law.
Everyday texture: smoke houses, bone markers, cold-ward charms, short funerals.
Trade: furs, preserved meat, iceglass, cold-ward craft.

Speech + naming: harsh consonants, clipped phrases. Places often include frost-, ash-, wind-, hold.

### Shattered Marches
The Marches are where history broke and never fully cleaned up. People live there anyway, because someone always lives where the money is dangerous.

Governance: salvage assemblies, ruin-claims, and “ownership by survival.”
Magic posture: pragmatic. Everyone knows residue; everyone has scars.
Everyday texture: scavenger tags, ash-lines on doorframes, improvised ward nails, markets selling items nobody admits to needing.
Trade: salvage, relic-adjacent scrap, ward components.

Speech + naming: slang-heavy; places favor material metaphors (“Blackglass,” “Riftshadow,” “Cinder”).

## REGION PROFILES (Local Atlas / Web Map)
These profiles support immediate play-space texture and naming.

### Frostveil Dominion (local)
Cold fortresses, ration law, wind that eats sound. Icewatch Hold is the anchor, and burial compliance is treated as engineering.

### Eryndor Confederacy (local)
Breadbasket politics: towns that cooperate because they remember what happens when they do not. Valemarch and Tharwick are the public faces.

### Verdantis Crownlands (local)
Deep green zones and old-growth infrastructure. Heartwood Spire is both landmark and warning.

### Ebonreach Covenant (local)
Wetlands, deltas, and “quiet law” — a culture built around what should not be said near water. Hollowgrave is a city that learned to live with ghosts without believing in them.

### Ashmarsh Principalities (local)
Residue-adjacent territory with salvage claims and shifting alliances. Cindralith is a market-town that measures value in survivable risk.

### Emberlands Imperium (local)
Heat, mineral routes, and engineered survival. Emberdeep is a town that lives because it keeps its wells and keeps its secrets.
