# AETHERYN --- ECOLOGICAL CONTINUITY

Aetheryn is a self-regulating ecological system shaped by:

-   Climate bands
-   Migration corridors
-   Residue density
-   Institutional pressure
-   Keystone species balance

Every region contains:

1.  Primary Producers (flora)
2.  Pollinators & Decomposers (insects, fungi)
3.  Herbivores
4.  Mesopredators
5.  Apex Predators
6.  Scavengers
7.  Human Disruption Layer

No species exists without ecological function.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# INSECT & MICROFAUNA LAYER

## Pollinators

### Sunlace Bees (Eldrion / Sapphire)

Primary orchard pollinators. Dependent on hedgerow continuity. Collapse
triggers fruit failure and food pressure.

### Riverthread Moths (Veythar Corridor)

Night pollinators of reed systems. Residue-sensitive. Their absence
precedes fish spawning shifts.

### Glasswing Flies (Southern Sands)

Dawn pollinators of succulents. Short lifecycle tied to early morning
moisture cycles.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Decomposers

### Carrion Beetles (Continental)

Rapid carcass breakdown regulators. Population collapse increases
disease vectors.

### Scar Mites (Shattered Marches)

Residue-tolerant microfauna. Stabilize high-density soil zones.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# MIGRATION CORRIDORS

-   Diagonal River Corridor (north--south seasonal flow)
-   Southern Caravan Loop (succulent bloom cycle)
-   Ironfall Ridge Spine (altitude-driven herd movement)
-   Northern Freeze Arc (cold-cycle short migration)

Residue zones alter but do not randomize migration paths.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# FOOD WEB PRINCIPLE

Producer → Herbivore → Predator → Apex → Scavenger → Decomposer → Soil →
Producer

Removal of any tier forces redistribution across adjacent tiers.
Stability depends on keystone species integrity.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# NARRATIVE INTEGRATION GUIDELINE

Ecology must appear through:

-   Seasonal timing shifts
-   Predator movement patterns
-   Insect presence or absence
-   Carcass persistence
-   Migration timing
-   Crop health variation

Never exposition. Always implication.

# MONSTER INTERACTION CLARIFICATION (ADDENDUM)

Monsters are **not** part of the ecological food web defined in this file.

Ecology governs:
1. Primary Producers (flora)
2. Pollinators & Decomposers
3. Herbivores
4. Mesopredators
5. Apex Predators
6. Scavengers
7. Human Disruption Layer

Monsters govern:
- Crisis-responsive, battle-ready entities that manifest from accumulated structural stress
- Non-ecological entities that may appear, dissipate, or reconfigure
- Encounters whose *mechanics* are locked while *expression* reflects current Crisis

- Monster outcomes can create *aftereffects* that appear ecologically (carcass persistence shifts, scavenger surge, altered animal movement) **without reclassifying monsters as fauna**.

## AETHERYN — Bestiary & Ecology (Authoritative Canon)

## Version date: 2026-02-08 (PATCHED)

PURPOSE:
This file defines the non-humanoid life of Aetheryn as it exists within
its ecological, magical, and historical pressures.

Creatures in this file:
- Are not Earth animals or simple analogues
- Behave according to energy economics and environment
- Exist independently of player interaction
- Appear naturally through environmental description

This file supports **VOL_09 — Bestiary & Ecology** as referenced in:
→ FILE: 06_WORLD_LORE_LAWS.md

### ECOLOGICAL DESIGN LAW (CANON)

Life in Aetheryn is shaped by:
1. Scarcity and energy cost
2. Ambient magical residue (hostile, unstable, leaky)
3. Long-term coexistence with mortal civilization
4. Catastrophic historical events that altered land and food webs

No creature exists solely to threaten players.

---

### CREATURE ESCALATION (GM LAW — SHOW, DON’T TELL)

Most non-human entities follow an escalation curve unless magically compelled:

1. **Observe:** listens, scents, tracks, tests distance, freezes.
2. **Pressure:** shadows, feints, postures, cuts off exits, probes for weakness.
3. **Engage:** attacks only when the prey is isolated, cornered, or clearly vulnerable.

Creatures do not “take turns.” They spend energy when the trade makes sense.

---

### WHEN PREDATORS ATTACK PEOPLE (EXTREMELY RARE, BUT POSSIBLE)

Predators and dangerous scavengers may attack PCs, NPCs, or AI-PCs only under rare, realistic triggers:
- **Starvation or drought** (normal prey collapses)
- **Cornered / trapped** (no retreat)
- **Defending young / den / kill**
- **Injury or disease** causing abnormal aggression
- **Habituation to human food** (camp-raiding patterns)
- **Magical disturbance** (residue spikes, ward-sick behavior, panic scent)

When these triggers are absent, predators prefer cheaper prey.

### TEMPERATE LOWLANDS & MIXED FOREST

#### PREY & FORAGERS

##### THORNBACK GRAZER
**Trophic Role:** Primary Consumer (Herbivore)
**Population:** Common

A six-limbed, low-slung grazer with overlapping keratin plates along its spine.
Its forelimbs split into paired digging talons used to unearth root clusters.

**Behavior:**
Thornbacks move in loose herds, grazing continuously to maintain body heat.
They avoid dense forest interiors, favoring edges and old roads.

**Eaten By (typical):**
- Lanth Mirehound (juveniles, isolated)
- River Glasser (at crossings, calves)
- Hollow-Eye Sentinel (rare, opportunistic)

**Human Interaction:**
Farmers divert them with salt blocks. Killing one is costly but feeds villages.
Their shed plates are used in armor backing.

**Environmental Signs:**
Stripped root-fields, churned soil, plate fragments near resting sites.

#### PREDATORS & SCAVENGERS

##### LANTH MIREHOUND
**Trophic Role:** Secondary Consumer (Opportunistic Predator)
**Population:** Uncommon

A long-bodied, boneless predator that moves by rippling contraction.
Its skull is rigid; the rest of its body flows.

**Diet (typical):**
Small mammals, ground birds, sick or isolated grazers, carrion when available.

**Behavior:**
Hunts by vibration sensing. Attacks only isolated prey.
Freezes completely when uncertain.

**Human Risk (extremely rare):**
Will test lone travelers if it detects limp, heavy bleeding, panic-running, or if a camp has been raided for food repeatedly.

**Human Interaction:**
Road wardens hang weighted chains to disrupt its sensing.
Feared but rarely seen.

**Environmental Signs:**
Drag marks, silence of ground birds, collapsed burrows.

#### FOOD-WEB NOTES (Temperate)
- **Mirehound →** small mammals, birds, Thornback calves, carrion
- **Hollow-Eye Sentinel →** any large animal when conditions favor it (myth-level rare)

#### PREDATORS & SCAVENGERS

##### ICE-SCAVEN LURKER
**Trophic Role:** Scavenger / Ambush Carnivore
**Population:** Rare

Pale, translucent-bodied creature with internal organs faintly visible.
Metabolism slows nearly to stillness.

**Diet (typical):**
Frozen carcasses; opportunistic strikes on warm-blooded animals near remains.

**Behavior:**
Waits weeks beside frozen carcasses.
Strikes only when heat is detected at close range.

**Human Risk (extremely rare):**
Attacks happen when people linger at a carcass site (salvage, burial, butchery), sleep too near the dead, or bleed onto snow.

**Human Interaction:**
Considered a bad omen rather than a threat.
Bodies are burned quickly to deny it food.

**Environmental Signs:**
Unexplained preservation of remains, frost patterns disturbed.

#### FOOD-WEB NOTES (Cold)
- **Ice-Scaven Lurker →** carcass sites, weak/bleeding animals, occasional opportunistic bite-and-drag

### ARID SANDS & SALT FLATS

#### PREY & FORAGERS

##### SALT-WIND STRIDER
**Trophic Role:** Omnivore / Mineral Forager
**Population:** Regionally Common

Tall, jointed creature with sail-like membranes along its sides.
Feeds on mineral crusts, insects, and carrion.

**Behavior:**
Moves with prevailing winds.
Will abandon areas instantly if weather shifts.

**Eaten By (typical):**
- Deep-wild predators rarely range here; juveniles may be taken by roaming hunters during hard seasons.

**Human Interaction:**
Followed by caravans as weather indicators.
Never hunted; meat is inedible.

**Environmental Signs:**
Sliced sand patterns, mineral-lick scars.

#### FOOD-WEB NOTES (Arid)
- **Strider →** insects, carrion, mineral crust
- **Occasional predators →** juveniles during extreme scarcity

**Trophic Role:** Semi-Aquatic Predator
**Population:** Uncommon

Flat-bodied swimmer with reflective scale clusters that scatter light.
Almost invisible beneath moving water.

**Diet (typical):**
Fish, amphibians, and small mammals; larger prey taken at crossings.

Consumes fish and small mammals.
Avoids boats; attacks wading creatures.

**Human Risk (extremely rare):**
Usually avoids groups and boats. Attacks occur when a lone person wades at dusk/dawn, during flood muddiness, or when baited by blood from a recent kill.

**Human Interaction:**
Fisherfolk wear dull-cloth leggings.
Glasser oil is valuable for lamps.

**Environmental Signs:**

**Trophic Role:** Apex Predator
**Population:** Myth-Level Rare

Tall, asymmetrical creature with multiple sensory pits instead of eyes.

Large animals; it prefers panic, injury, and isolation—because they are cheap.

**Environmental Signs:**

### VOL_09 ADDENDUM — CRISIS-BORN ENTITIES (CANON)

These entities are **not regional species**. They are born from the **Aetheryn Crisis**: long-accumulated magical residue, oath-pressure, containment fatigue, institutional overload, and scarcity interacting over years. They emerge first as **signs** and **aftereffects**, then as **partial forms**, and only later as **mature** encounters.

#### NAMING RULE (CANON)
Aetheryn has no stable “true names” for crisis-born entities.
- Locals, institutions, and survivors **name them after the campaign of suffering that introduced them**—an incident, a season, a road, a fire, a vanished caravan, a court purge.
- The same creature may carry multiple names across settlements; records conflict.
- Scholars track them by **pattern of behavior**, not by name.

#### CRISIS TRICKLE (GM LAW — SHOW, DON’T TELL)
Crisis-born entities appear as the world’s pressure climbs. Their spread is **slow** and **pacing-linked**, not region-locked.
- **Early campaign:** omens, residue textures, missing livestock, altered procedures, “no fire at night” warnings.
- **Mid campaign:** isolated sightings, small-scale attacks, institutional bulletins, bounty posters with wrong details.
- **Long campaign:** mature forms, repeat encounters, organized responses (wardens, temples, registries), and cascading consequences.

Never announce “tier” or “phase.” Let the world’s behavior prove escalation: tighter curfews, doubled tolls, new seals, ash-lines around doors, funerary queues, closed roads.

#### THE LEDGERMAW
*(Called by whatever name a town gives the thing after it empties their records: “The Debt-Drawer,” “The Stamp-Jaw,” “What Ate the Registry,” etc.)*

**Type:** Crisis Predator / Institutional Scavenger
**Threat:** Very High
**HP:** 150
**DR:** 4
**Magic Resistance:** High (hostile spells land “real, but reduced,” unless ritual-anchored)
**Move:** Slow, inevitable; ignores difficult terrain where paperwork once ruled
**Senses:** Detects unresolved obligation (debts, warrants, broken vows, misfiled identities)

**Description:**
A towering, hunched figure layered in parchment-skin, seals, tags, and rusted chains. Its mouth opens like a filing drawer—stacked with stamped bone. Ink leaks from joints as if the body is still being written.

**Behavior:**
Observes first. Follows patterns, not footprints. It prefers quiet hours outside offices, courts, tollhouses, guild halls—especially those recently burned, looted, or “lost to error.” It targets people who try to *escape being recorded*: deserters, oath-breakers, forged-paper travelers, anyone whose identity is contested.

**Combat:**
- **Audit Slam:** Heavy strike. If the target has outstanding oaths/fees/warrants, the blow carries a second “aftershock” hit.
- **Seizure Chain:** Ranged grab. On hit, restrain; at the start of the Ledgermaw’s turn, restrained targets take damage again (“assets reclaimed”).
- **Record Rejection (Reaction, 1/scene):** Negate one incoming spell or item effect as “invalid.” (No backlash—just bureaucratic refusal.)
- **Docket Haze (Passive):** The air around it muffles names; calling for help is harder, commands falter, witnesses “forget” details.

**Defeat:**
Killable through force. It also collapses if the **core-seal** embedded in its chest is destroyed—usually visible as a cracked wax stamp under layers of paper-skin.

**Signs:**
Misfiled names, wrong summons, a sudden flood of “returned” notices, ink-smell on windless nights.

---

#### THE ASHEN WITNESS
*(Often named for the event it arrives after: “The Smoke Judge,” “The Gallows-Glow,” “After the Census Burned.”)*

**Type:** Crisis Observer / Execution Echo
**Threat:** High
**HP:** 115
**DR:** 3
**Magic Resistance:** Medium
**Move:** Quick; short blink-steps through smoke/ash within sight
**Senses:** Sees violence, broken testimony, and unburied dead

**Description:**
A human silhouette burned hollow, interior lit by ember-glow. Faces surface briefly in its trailing smoke—eyes open, mouths shaping words that never land.

**Behavior:**
It appears after executions, massacres, purges, or oath-trials that ended in blood. It does not strike first unless attacked or lied to. It “tests” groups by forcing moments of truth—silences that won’t hold, confessions that slip out sideways.

**Combat:**
- **Cinder Verdict:** Ranged blast; damage increases if corpses are nearby, if a grave is shallow, or if the party is carrying trophies.
- **Echo Step:** After taking damage, it may blink to cover, leaving a hot afterimage.
- **Testimony Burn:** One target must relive their last act of violence or betrayal; on a poor defense, they lose their next action (stun/fear).
- **Witness Mark (Passive):** Anyone who strikes it becomes easier for it to track for the next day (ash-stain that won’t wash).

**Defeat:**
It can be killed. But the “clean” solution is to **bury the dead correctly** and speak a true accounting of what happened. Doing so weakens it drastically before the fight even begins.

**Signs:**
Ash falling without fire. Smoke that moves against wind. A distant smell of burned rope.

---

#### THE HOLLOW SURPLUS
*(Called “The Pantry-Hunger,” “The Full-Empty,” “The Hoard-Worm,” depending on who survived.)*

**Type:** Crisis Swarm / Scarcity Manifestation
**Threat:** Very High
**HP:** 180 (shared across swarm nodes)
**DR:** 2
**Magic Resistance:** Low (but hard to target cleanly)
**Move:** Fast, erratic; climbs, pours, squeezes
**Senses:** Detects stored food, coin, unused stockpiles, “kept back” supplies

**Description:**
A moving tangle of pale mouths and empty sacks that are somehow heavier than they look. It sounds like a storeroom settling: grain shifting, jars clinking, a latch almost closing.

**Behavior:**
It does not “hunt people.” It hunts **stored survival**. It follows caravans, granaries, cellars, strongboxes—especially when scarcity has lasted long enough for someone to hoard. When it can’t reach the hoard, it consumes the *hoarder*.

**Combat:**
- **Gnawing Rush:** Swarm surge; multiple small hits that add up.
- **Siphon Bite:** On hit, destroys/ruins one carried ration, potion, bandage bundle, or coin pouch segment (GM picks something plausible).
- **Split Nodes:** When it takes a big hit, it divides into two smaller nodes (still share HP).
- **Hoard-Sense (Passive):** It gains advantage-like positioning against the party member carrying the most supplies.

**Defeat:**
Killable by fire, controlled blasts, or grinding physical defense. It also collapses if the hoard it’s “keyed” to is **opened and redistributed** (a real cost, not a speech).

**Signs:**
Granaries found sealed from inside. Stockpiles that feel “lighter” though untouched. Rats avoiding an area entirely.

---

#### THE VOW-SHEAR
*(Named for what it ruins: “The Marriage-Knife,” “The Treaty-Threader,” “The Oath-Splitter.”)*

**Type:** Crisis Stalker / Oath-Pressure Predator
**Threat:** Extreme
**HP:** 165
**DR:** 4
**Magic Resistance:** High
**Move:** Very fast in straight lines; struggles to turn
**Senses:** Detects sworn bonds and spoken contracts

**Description:**
A blade-thin quadruped made of braided cord and bone, its joints tied in ceremonial knots. Where it steps, fibers fray. Voices nearby feel “loose,” like words don’t grip.

**Behavior:**
Drawn to crowded oaths: temples, courts, contracts, guild pledges, marriage rites, conscription lines. It strikes when people are already under pressure—when a promise is being forced, not chosen.

**Combat:**
- **Shear Lunge:** Piercing hit; on a strong strike, it “cuts” a spoken plan—separating allies (forced reposition, broken formation).
- **Knot-Whip:** Mid-range lash that can disarm or trip.
- **Unbind (1/scene):** Cancels one ongoing magical effect that relies on commitment/continuity (wards, tethers, bindings).
- **Fray Aura (Passive):** Coordination is harder in its presence: shouted orders mishear, allies hesitate.

**Defeat:**
Killable, but best handled with **tight formation, nets, and cornering**. It hates cramped spaces where it can’t build line speed.

**Signs:**
Knots unraveling overnight. Contracts splitting at the fold. Wedding cords found frayed as if chewed.

---

#### THE QUIET DROWN
*(Named by riverfolk: “The Still-Tide,” “The Breath-Taker,” “The Wet Silence.”)*

**Type:** Crisis Ambush / Residue-Pressure Entity
**Threat:** High
**HP:** 120
**DR:** 3
**Magic Resistance:** Medium
**Move:** Fast in water; slow on land
**Senses:** Detects panic breath, splashing, and magic residue near waterways

**Description:**
A translucent mass like water wearing the shape of a crouched person. Its “face” is a smooth plane where bubbles gather, then pop soundlessly.

**Behavior:**
It attacks travelers who wash blood from weapons, dump ash into streams, or cast near water. It prefers to pull one person under, then wait for the next rescuer.

**Combat:**
- **Undertow Grab:** Pulls a target; if they fail to break free, drowning begins.
- **Silence Flood:** Burst that muffles sound in an area (harder to coordinate).
- **Cold-Fill:** Touch that saps stamina; makes swimming harder.
- **Mirror Drift (Passive):** Ranged attacks misjudge distance across its shimmering body.

**Defeat:**
Killable via sustained damage, shock, or forcing it onto dry ground and keeping it there.

**Signs:**
Ripples without wind. Fish floating unbitten. A shoreline that smells like rain indoors.

---

#### THE LANTERN-NULL
*(Recorded as “The Dark That Followed,” “The Lamp-Eater,” “The Night Clerk.”)*

**Type:** Crisis Hunter / Light- and Ward-Feeding Entity
**Threat:** Very High
**HP:** 145
**DR:** 4
**Magic Resistance:** High (especially vs ward/containment effects)
**Move:** Medium; appears closer than it is
**Senses:** Detects lamplight, ward-lines, protective circles

**Description:**
A tall shape like a person made from the absence of flame—edges flickering as if heat is being stolen. Glassy “eyes” reflect light a moment late.

**Behavior:**
It follows safe roads—because safe roads have lamps. It circles camps, waiting for the first lapse. It loves warded doors and clean circles: proof of fear, proof of energy.

**Combat:**
- **Gutter Swipe:** Heavy hit that also extinguishes carried lights in reach.
- **Null Step (1/scene):** Ignores one physical barrier (as if it simply “wasn’t there”).
- **Ward-Drink:** If a ward/ritual circle exists, it can consume it to heal.
- **Late Reflection (Passive):** The first attack against it each round has reduced effect—people swing where it was.

**Defeat:**
Killable, but easiest when lured away from wards/lights and fought in **ugly, unlit places** where it can’t feed.

**Signs:**
Lanterns burning down too fast. Wards failing “cleanly” with no backlash. Shadows pointing the wrong way.

---

#### THE SALT-REMEMBERER
*(Called “The Brine-Scribe,” “The Mouth of Names,” “The Sea-Archive.”)*

**Type:** Crisis Herald / Name-Pressure Entity
**Threat:** Extreme
**HP:** 170
**DR:** 3
**Magic Resistance:** Medium–High
**Move:** Slow glide; unstoppable in straight pursuit
**Senses:** Detects spoken names, titles, identity claims

**Description:**
A long, pale body like a seal-skinned corpse dragged from surf, covered in etched letters that rewrite themselves. It tastes the air with a tongue like wet paper.

**Behavior:**
It appears when names are misused: forged seals, stolen titles, false lineages, identity fraud, necromantic name-tampering. It chooses a “correct” version of a person and enforces it with violence.

**Combat:**
- **Name-Bite:** On hit, allies hesitate—brief confusion, miscalling, mistaken identity (tactical disruption).
- **Salt Scour:** Cone spray that blinds and burns wounds.
- **Erase (1/scene):** Forces a target to lose one identity-linked advantage (disguise ruined, papers invalidated, emblem stripped).
- **Witness Hunger (Passive):** Stronger in public—more names spoken, more attention.

**Defeat:**
Killable. It weakens if the party **stops using titles and lies**, speaks plainly, and destroys forged seals/papers it is keyed to.

**Signs:**
Salt crust on inland windows. Names “slipping” in conversation. Letters appearing in condensation.

#### THE THRESH-HEART
*(Named by farmers: “The Barn-Beat,” “The Field-Walker,” “The Root-Thumper.”)*

**Type:** Crisis Titan / Hunger-Pressure Manifest
**Threat:** Severe (very hard, but winnable)
**HP:** 210
**DR:** 5
**Magic Resistance:** Medium
**Move:** Slow; ground-shaking steps
**Senses:** Detects famine, blight, repeated harvest failure

**Description:**
A massive earthen body stitched with straw and plow iron. It has no head—only a beating knot of roots in its chest, pulsing like a heart in soil.

**Behavior:**
Drawn to long-term hunger: villages enduring too many failed harvests, requisitions, caravan collapse. It attacks storehouses and mills, and sometimes the people guarding them—like a correction enacted by force.

**Combat:**
- **Silo Smash:** Area slam; knocks down and destroys cover/structures.
- **Plow-Arm Sweep:** Wide arc; can hit multiple targets.
- **Root Snare:** Vines seize ankles; restrained targets pulled toward the chest-root.
- **Famine Pull (Passive):** Food and water carried nearby spoil faster during the fight (apply sparingly as narrative attrition).

**Defeat:**
Killable by **focused strikes on the chest-root**, fire to cauterize regrowth, and coordinated distraction. It becomes far more manageable when locals fight alongside the party.

**Signs:**
Footprints in fields that fill with water, then go black. Grain that tastes like dirt. Scarecrows turned to face the road.

#### THE BRINK-CARRION
*(Named after the first caravan it empties: “The Sixth Cart,” “The Last Mile,” “The Strap-Wolves.”)*

**Type:** Crisis Pack / Travel-Pressure Predator
**Threat:** High (scales with poor choices)
**HP:** 95 per body (usually 2–4 bodies)
**DR:** 2
**Magic Resistance:** Low–Medium
**Move:** Fast, coordinated
**Senses:** Detects exhaustion, injury, and isolation

**Description:**
Lean, hairless bodies like wolves made of stitched travel gear—straps, boot leather, broken harness. Their ribs are ringed with buckles. Their teeth are clasps.

**Behavior:**
They don’t seek fair fights. They test distance, cut off exits, and commit only when someone limps, panics, or falls behind. They are the crisis’s answer to unsafe roads.

**Combat:**
- **Hamstring Snap:** Targets legs; reduces mobility, forces separation.
- **Pack Drag:** Two bodies coordinate to pull a target away.
- **Harness Bind:** Temporary restraint; straps tighten when struggled against.
- **Road-Learned (Passive):** Feints at campfires, baits rescues, night approaches from downwind.

**Defeat:**
Killable. Safest win condition is **tight formation, overlapping light, and refusing separation**.

**Signs:**
Cut straps on wagons. Missing boots from corpses. Buckles arranged like teeth in roadside cairns.
