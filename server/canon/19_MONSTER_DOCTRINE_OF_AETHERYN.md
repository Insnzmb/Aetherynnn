# AETHERYN — MONSTER DOCTRINE

## I. SEPARATION LAW

Animals:
- Exist independently of Crisis
- Part of trophic chain
- Reproductive
- Energy-bound

Monsters:
- Crisis-responsive
- Non-ecological entities
- Battle-ready by design
- May appear, dissipate, or reconfigure
- Do not require breeding logic

Animals stabilize.
Monsters destabilize.

---

# II. MONSTER ONTOLOGY

A monster in Aetheryn is:
A combat-capable manifestation of accumulated structural stress.

They are born from:
- Institutional overreach
- Residue imbalance
- Ideological polarization
- War pressure
- Ritual misalignment
- Environmental shock layered with Crisis

---

# III. LOCKED ARCHETYPE TABLE

1. Skirmisher — Mobility pressure
2. Hunter — Isolation punisher
3. Juggernaut — Line breaker
4. Controller — Field denial
5. Executioner — Burst finisher
6. Harrow Swarm — Multi-body pressure

All archetypes:

- No range bands
- Discrete outcomes only
- No scaling with Crisis

---

# IV. CRISIS EXPRESSION ENGINE

Crisis always alters **appearance, symbolism, and environmental signature**.

**Core statlines never change.** (HP, DEF/armor value, attack bonus, damage expression, and archetype role remain stable.)

However, at higher Crisis the wandering rupture may attach **universal Expression Tags** that are:
- fixed (no scaling numbers),
- identical across all monsters,
- and treated as environmental rupture behavior rather than “monster leveling.”

Crisis 0–2: Latent Distortion  
Crisis 3–5: Visible Fracture  
Crisis 6–8: Open Instability *(Expression Tags may apply)*  
Crisis 9–10: Mythic Rupture *(Expression Tags almost always apply)*  

## Universal Expression Tag — [Volatile] (High Crisis)

At **Crisis 6+**, monsters commonly manifest as **Ruptured**: they gain **[Volatile]**.

**[Volatile]:** When the monster dies, its body ruptures into unstable residue. The GM expresses this as a short-range hazard (default: **d6** harm/effect in an adjacent zone or immediate radius, using the active engine’s hazard conventions).

This does not change the monster’s core statline; it changes what the rupture does to the scene.

# V. BEHAVIORAL INTELLIGENCE LAYER

Monsters prioritize:
- Densest energy clusters
- Visible authority
- Open wounds (physical or institutional)

Each archetype has tactical personality:
- Skirmisher tests perimeter
- Juggernaut fixates on obstacles
- Hunter isolates prey
- Controller shapes terrain
- Executioner waits for vulnerability
- Crisis Avatar forces phase shifts

# VII. MONSTER SCAR SYSTEM

Defeated monsters leave one scar:

1. Residue Scar
2. Institutional Scar
3. Rumor Scar
4. Ecological Scar
5. Territorial Scar

Three matching scars trigger Minor Regional Rewrite.

# VIII. LONG-HORIZON MONSTER EVOLUTION MODEL

If a region experiences repeated monster manifestations tied to similar pressure axes:

Stage 1: Recurring Expression Pattern
Stage 2: Stable Archetype Frequency Bias
Stage 3: Emergent Regional Monster Identity

Evolution affects narrative form, not mechanics.

---

# IX. CRISIS AVATAR SUCCESSION TREE

If a Crisis Avatar is defeated while Crisis remains high:

Successor form may manifest reflecting unresolved dominant pressure.
Succession preserves mechanical template but shifts symbolic embodiment.

## Stat Block Format (Minimal)

- **HP**: hit points / durability pool
- **DEF**: defense target to hit (engine-mappable)
- **ATK**: attack modifier (engine-mappable)
- **DMG**: default harm expression (engine-mappable)
- **Behavior / Trigger**: tactical personality; deterministic tells

If the active engine uses different labels, map as follows:
- DEF ↔ the engine’s “to-hit target” for attacks against the creature.
- ATK ↔ the engine’s attack roll modifier.
- DMG ↔ harm expression (dice or fixed harm), as used by the current combat layer.

## Universal Crisis Note

- Crisis 0–5: Standard Form
- Crisis 6+: Ruptured Form (adds **[Volatile]** on death; see Section IV)

## 1) Hollow Husk (Skirmisher)
*Manifestation of starvation/neglect.*
- **HP:** 8 | **DEF:** 12 | **ATK:** +3 | **DMG:** d20 (Claw)
- **Behavior:** Swarms. Flees if alone.
- **Trigger:** Targets anyone with `cond:Exhausted` first.

## 2) Iron-Bound Sentinel (Juggernaut)
*Manifestation of rigid authority/law.*
- **HP:** 25 | **DEF:** 16 | **ATK:** +5 | **DMG:** d20 (Maul)
- **Behavior:** Slow. Ignores cover. Breaks lines.
- **Trigger:** Fixates on the best-armored / most-armed target.

## 3) Whisper-Thief (Controller)
*Manifestation of paranoia/secrets.*
- **HP:** 12 | **DEF:** 14 | **ATK:** +4 | **DMG:** d20 (Mind-Spike) + Silence-effect
- **Behavior:** Stays at range. Uses concealment.
- **Trigger:** Attempts to suppress speech/spellcasting first.

## 4) Glass-Jaw Horror (Executioner)
*Manifestation of sudden violence.*
- **HP:** 15 | **DEF:** 10 | **ATK:** +7 | **DMG:** d20 (Glass-Blade)
- **Behavior:** Ambush burst. Low defense, high lethality.
- **Trigger:** Uses Shatter Strike when target is below half vitality.

## 5) Rot-Weaver (Harrow Swarm)
*Manifestation of decay.*
- **HP:** 20 (Swarm) | **DEF:** 11 | **ATK:** auto-zone | **DMG:** d20/round (Acid zone)
- **Behavior:** Creates hazardous terrain.
- **Trigger:** Splits into two 10 HP swarms if struck by slashing harm (engine-mappable).

## 6) Ember-Scourge (Hunter)
*Manifestation of uncontrolled fire.*
- **HP:** 18 | **DEF:** 13 | **ATK:** +5 | **DMG:** d20 (Burn-Lash) + Ignite-effect
- **Behavior:** Circles edges. Herds prey into hazards.
- **Trigger:** Sets the environment on fire to constrain movement.

## 7) Aether-Jackal (Scavenger)
*Manifestation of magic waste.*
- **HP:** 10 | **DEF:** 15 | **ATK:** +3 | **DMG:** d20 (Bite) + Drain-effect
- **Behavior:** Steals mana/relic charge rather than committing to a kill.
- **Trigger:** Flees immediately after a successful bite to “digest” energy.

## 8) Stone-Grief Golem (Juggernaut)
*Manifestation of mourning.*
- **HP:** 40 | **DEF:** 18 | **ATK:** +4 | **DMG:** d20 (Slam)
- **Behavior:** Passive until provoked or disrupted.
- **Trigger:** Enters Rage (more aggressive) when an adjacent ally is killed.

## 9) False-Face Siren (Controller)
*Manifestation of betrayal.*
- **HP:** 14 | **DEF:** 13 | **ATK:** +4 | **DMG:** special (Lure)
- **Behavior:** Mimics friendly voices; isolates targets.
- **Trigger:** Attempts to pull a target adjacent, then stun/lock them in place (engine-mappable).

## 10) Rupture-Lord (Crisis Avatar — Boss)
*Manifestation of total collapse.*
- **HP:** 60 | **DEF:** 17 | **ATK:** +8 | **DMG:** d20 (Void-Cleave)
- **Behavior:** Warps the scene; phase shifts.
- **Trigger (Phase 2):** At half HP, gravity inverts; all movement costs double (engine-mappable).
