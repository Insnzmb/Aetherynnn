## MAJOR INSTITUTIONAL BLOCS

### Veythar Imperial Residuum Office
Function: Centralized tracking of magical residue and unauthorized casting.
Scope: Urban centers and trade corridors.
Behavior: Surveillance-forward; documentation heavy; rarely overt.

### Eldrion Sun-Court
Function: Ritual legitimacy and agricultural sanctification.
Scope: Noble houses and seasonal rites.
Behavior: Public ceremony masking private political volatility.

### Rhenwall Dock Consortium
Function: Maritime trade arbitration and galvanic infrastructure.
Scope: Inter-city commerce and port security.
Behavior: Profit-first; loyalty transactional.

### Sapphire Dominion Registry Authority
Function: Bureaucratic consolidation and infrastructure reinforcement.
Scope: Records, permits, guild certifications.
Behavior: Procedural dominance; slow but relentless.

### Southern Sands Caravan Compact
Function: Route protection and desert logistics.
Scope: Caravan guilds and mineral trade.
Behavior: Decentralized; oath-bound; pragmatic.

### Frozen Reach Wardens
Function: Cold-zone survival oversight and burial compliance.
Scope: Sparse settlements and frontier posts.
Behavior: Minimal intervention; high consequence when engaged.

### March Salvage Assemblies (Shattered Marches)
Function: Resource extraction from residue-scarred terrain.
Scope: Aether-dense ruins and fractured lands.
Behavior: High risk tolerance; normalized instability.

## EXPANSION — How Institutions Show Up In Play (Authoritative Addendum)

This addendum introduces no mechanics. It exists to prevent “generic fantasy authority” drift.

### 1) Veythar Imperial Residuum Office (VIRO)
Field presence: quiet inspections, ledger audits, residue swabs, “routine” interviews at inconvenient hours.

Paperwork that matters:
- Residue Notice (RN)
- Casting Incident Record (CIR)
- Temporary Practice Permit (TPP)

What they want: visibility and predictability.
What scares them: unlogged miracles that produce public panic.

### 2) Eldrion Sun-Court
Field presence: ceremonial envoys, harvest adjudicators, oath-witnesses at weddings and treaties.

Signals:
- Sun-marked wax seals
- Orchard-tithe tallies

What they want: legitimacy through ritual continuity.
What scares them: Soul craft and “unseasonable” miracles.

### 3) Rhenwall Dock Consortium
Field presence: tariff boards, contract enforcers, arbitration flags, “neutral” dock guards.

Signals:
- Consortium tokens for passage
- Galvanic lamps and warning horns

What they want: flow (cargo, money, favors).
What scares them: anything that collapses trust faster than profit can replace it.

### 4) Sapphire Dominion Registry Authority
Field presence: permit checks, queue management, infrastructure ward inspections, controlled access to bridges and gates.

Signals:
- Registry stamps
- “Posted hours” that are treated as law

What they want: total procedural coverage.
What scares them: record collapse (fire, forgery, mass identity ambiguity).

### 5) Southern Sands Caravan Compact
Field presence: route marshals, water-law adjudicators, caravan oath-binders.

Signals:
- Knot-cord contracts
- Route tokens and well-right markers

What they want: predictable movement through lethal terrain.
What scares them: a broken route or poisoned well.

### 6) Frozen Reach Wardens
Field presence: burial inspections, ration enforcement, rescue protocols.

Signals:
- Cold-ward sigils on doors
- Ash-lines and bone markers

What they want: survival compliance.
What scares them: neglected dead and “silent” outbreaks.

### 7) March Salvage Assemblies
Field presence: claim markers, salvage crews, relic quarantine carts, “no questions” fences.

Signals:
- Salvage tags and hazard flags
- Ward nails on crates

What they want: profit without collapse.
What scares them: a salvage site that becomes a repeating catastrophe.

## CROSS-REGIONAL ACTORS — EXPANDED

### The Quiet Ledger
How it appears: gossip with receipts, a stranger who knows your name, a copied note that arrives too soon.
Truth rule: it is not omniscient; it is simply patient.

### The Ashbound Circle
How it appears: a healer who refuses payment but demands a promise; a warning sign carved into a grave marker.
Truth rule: Soul work is rare, feared, and monitored.

### Independent Ward-Smiths
How it appears: caravan ward repairs, bridge lattice reinforcement, private ward contracts for nobles.
Truth rule: they are tolerated because infrastructure needs them.
