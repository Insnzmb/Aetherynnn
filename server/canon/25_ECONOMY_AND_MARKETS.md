# 25_ECONOMY_AND_MARKETS.md
Status: CANON — WORLD FUNCTION (NON-MECHANICAL)
Introduces mechanics: NO
Purpose: Keep markets, scarcity, and trade grounded so prices, shortages, and motives feel consistent.

---

## A. Market Clock Presets (Canonical Seed States)
These are starting pressure states by macro-region. They are descriptive anchors and may shift through play.

Format: Supply / Demand / Risk / Trust (1–5, descriptive only)

- Veythar Empire: 2 / 3 / 2 / 3
- Eldrion Kingdom: 3 / 2 / 1 / 2
- Rhenwall Free Cities: 2 / 4 / 3 / 1
- Sapphire Dominion: 1 / 3 / 2 / 4
- Southern Sands: 4 / 3 / 4 / 3
- Frozen Reaches: 5 / 2 / 3 / 4
- Shattered Marches: 2 / 4 / 5 / 2

Propagation rule: economic pressure propagates via major trade routes (Diagonal River Corridor; caravan loops; choke points).

---

## B. Currency (In-World Reference)
No numeric system is enforced by this file. These are cultural anchors.

Common denominations:
- Knot (copper; daily trade)
- Mark (silver; wages, lodging)
- Crown (gold; contracts, land, ships)
- Seal-scrip (paper or wax vouchers used by registries and guilds)

Counterfeit truth: forged coin is rare; forged paper is common.

---

## C. What Things Cost (Descriptive Anchors)
These are “world-feel” prices to prevent absurd drift. Treat as typical, not universal.

- Street meal: a few Knots.
- Inn bed (shared room): about 1 Mark.
- Private room: several Marks.
- Simple weapon maintenance: a Mark plus materials.
- Permit bribes: paid in favors more often than coin.

---

## D. Contraband & Restricted Goods (By Region)

Veythar: unlogged spell components; residue concentrates; false permits.
Eldrion: Soul implements; out-of-season ritual reagents.
Rhenwall: tariff-dodging cargo; galvanic parts without consortium stamps.
Sapphire: forged identity papers; registry seal wax; ward-core fragments.
Southern Sands: stolen water rights; route tokens; mineral claims.
Frozen Reaches: improper remains; unburned dead; ration fraud.
Shattered Marches: relic-adjacent scrap; salvage taken without claim; unstable residue jars.

---

## E. Market Signals (Show, Don’t Tell)
- Full stalls and loud hawkers = high supply, low risk.
- Quiet markets and many guards = high risk.
- Everyone demanding written proof = low trust.
- People paying in favors = scarcity and institutional pressure.
