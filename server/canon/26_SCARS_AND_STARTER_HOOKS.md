# 26_SCARS_AND_STARTER_HOOKS.md
Status: CANON — WORLD GRAVITY (PLAY SEEDS)
Introduces mechanics: NO
Purpose: Bind history to geography and give the GM stable “first quests” without railroading.

---

## A. Canonical Scars (World-Scale)

### 1) The Black Orchard (−1666, sealed)
Truth: a miracle-land fed thousands; then the dead sprouted with the crops. The land was sealed and erased.
Modern consequence: quarantine doctrine and “burn-before-bloom” paranoia in agrarian regions.

### 2) The Ash Census (−1987)
Truth: regions began counting magic use. Obscurity ended.
Modern consequence: licenses, registries, guild charters; uncounted casting is treated as structural threat.

### 3) The Burning of Hesh (−972)
Truth: a false resurrection was exposed; the city burned itself.
Modern consequence: resurrection taboos and burial compliance rituals.

### 4) The War of Seven Witnesses (−1237)
Truth: incompatible divine testimony; states chose witnesses and went to war.
Modern consequence: skepticism toward prophets; institutionalized containment of “public miracles.”

### 5) The Ledger Fire (+88)
Truth: a provincial archive burned; debts vanished, grudges did not.
Modern consequence: registry hardening; fear of record loss; rumor economies.

### 6) The Three-Day Quiet (−412)
Truth: all birds fell silent across three regions.
Modern consequence: omen folklore that still shapes travel behavior.

## B. Local Scars (Web Map Theater)
These are localized, playable scars that tie into the web map anchors.

1) Ashwind Pass: a choke point where “paper law” meets weather. Curfews appear after residue storms.
2) Wraithwater Delta (local Ebonreach coast): shallow graves and old testimony; smoke that moves wrong.
3) Southern Rift Crossing (road gate between Cindralith ↔ Emberdeep): contested routes, toll violence, vanished caravans.
4) Heartwood Spire shadowline: the forest’s “stillness” line—locals speak softer past it.

## C. Starter Nudge Quests (Non-Railroad)
Each hook is a nudge, not a mandate. Use whichever matches the player’s starting location.

### Veythar / Paper-Law Hooks
1) A travel permit is misfiled under the PC’s name; an inspector arrives with the wrong confidence.
2) A courier offers coin to carry a sealed packet that should not exist.
3) A registry clerk begs the PC to retrieve a ledger page before it gets “corrected.”

### Eldrion / Harvest-Rite Hooks
1) Orchard bees vanish overnight; the Sun-Court wants a quiet explanation.
2) A wedding oath is delayed “for safety.” The couple asks for help.
3) A shrine’s wax seals are found softened without heat.

### Rhenwall / Dock-Hub Hooks
1) A ship refuses to dock because the harbor lights “blink wrong.”
2) A tariff marker changes between morning and noon.
3) A dockworker claims a crate “weighs like a name.”

### Sapphire / Registry Hooks
1) A bridge permit suddenly requires an impossible ancestor.
2) A ward lattice hums at night and the inspectors won’t say why.
3) A missing stamp makes a person legally dead.

### Southern Sands / Route Hooks
1) A well-right marker is moved; a caravan is about to start a war over it.
2) A route token appears in the PC’s pack with no memory of where it came from.
3) A sandstorm reveals a caravan that died last week—still walking.

### Frozen Reaches / Burial Hooks
1) A body will not stay burned; the wardens want witnesses, not rumors.
2) A cold-ward charm cracks in a pattern older than the village.
3) Something drags graves open without taking the dead.

### Shattered Marches / Salvage Hooks
1) A salvage tag is nailed to the PC’s door.
2) A ruin-claim map is sold twice with different ink.
3) A ward-smith refuses to repair a lattice unless a certain scrap is returned.
