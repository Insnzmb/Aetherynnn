# AETHERYN CONSOLIDATED AMENDMENT

## Combat Doctrine + Spell Architecture + Relic Doctrine

---

## I. Combat Doctrine Addendum

**Tone:** Fast & Lethal  
- Most combats resolve in **2–4 rounds**.  
- High-margin success is **decisive**.  
- Positioning and timing matter more than inflated HP.  
- No infinite stacking bonuses.  
- No damage-sponge enemies.

### Resource System

**Casters**
- Medium Mana Pool (**8–10 baseline**).
- Spells cost **1–5 mana** depending on tier.
- Hidden **Arcane Strain** builds with rapid casting.
- After several spells in short succession, backlash risk increases.
- Strain resets gradually after combat.

**Martial**
- Stamina Pool (**10–12 baseline**).
- Visible fatigue as stamina lowers.
- Strong zone control and engagement authority.
- Can attempt spell disruption at close range.

**Ranged**
- Lower stamina cost per attack.
- High precision, low control authority.
- Increased vulnerability when engaged in melee.

### Death & Resurrection

- Death is common in combat.
- Resurrection is **Forbidden Knowledge**.
- Requires:
  1. Soul ritual
  2. Relic sacrifice
  3. Institutional sanction
- Return always causes transformation (examples):
  - Weakened
  - Changed
  - Marked by divine notice
  - Environmental echo
- Rare anomalies possible (double presence, bleed-through, delayed instability).
- No resurrection is consequence-free.

## II. Spell Architecture Framework (Updated)

### Six Towers (Core Pillars / Player-Facing)

1. **Fire** — combustion, heat, forceful expression  
2. **Water** — flow, phase, pressure, cold/brine  
3. **Air** — breath, sound, storm, static  
4. **Earth** — stone, metal, weight, rust  
5. **Soul** — life/death adjacency, identity, bonds, memory  
6. **Aether** — space, wards, reality-lattice, residue craft  

### Non-Core Domain (Not a Tower)

- **Verdant** is not a tower.  
  Verdant is a **synthesis domain**: growth & living change expressed through multi-pillar interaction  
  (commonly **Earth + Water**, with **Soul pressure** as the identity-binding constraint).  
  Access is rare, political, relic-mediated, or crisis-adjacent.

> **Fate is removed as a castable pillar.** “Coincidence pressure / mythic convergence” is handled by engine doctrine,
> scars, and GM escalation—not spells.

### Spell Structure

Each spell includes:
- Name
- What it does (plain language)
- Resolution method (3d6 for actions; d20 for damage)
- Risk (the balancing hook)

### Spell Lanes

- **Flash (F):** immediate, cinematic effects.
- **Tactical (T):** control, positioning, constraints, or battlefield shaping.

### Tier Progression (Tower Extended to Tier 5)

Baseline tower volume is **50 spells**:
- **Tier 1:** 10 spells (cost 1 mana)
- **Tier 2:** 12 spells (cost 2 mana)
- **Tier 3:** 12 spells (cost 3 mana)
- **Tier 4:** 8 spells (cost 4 mana)
- **Tier 5:** 8 spells (cost 5 mana)

**No spell:**
- Modifies dice size permanently
- Stacks flat numeric bonuses
- Bypasses discrete resolution

## III. Relic Doctrine Addendum

**Core Principle:** Relics are fragments of divine intent. Most have hidden consequences.

### Impact Tiers

**Tier I — Curious Relics**
- Minor effects
- Mostly safe
- Occasionally researched

**Tier II — Potent Relics**
- Strong leverage
- Hidden cost

**Tier III — Dangerous Relics**
- Conflict-shaping
- Major consequences

**Tier IV — World-Shaping Relics**
- Era-defining
- Extremely rare

### Relic Structure (Engine Layer)

Each relic contains:
- Primary Effect (visible)
- Echo Vector (hidden)
- Residual Mark (long-term effect)
- Discovery Path (rumor, clergy, academia, etc.)

**Relics:**
- Never stack numeric bonuses
- Never inflate base dice
- Always leave narrative consequence
