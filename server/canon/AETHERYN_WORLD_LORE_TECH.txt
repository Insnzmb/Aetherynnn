# AETHERYN_WORLD_LORE_TECH.aeth (Section: GODSLAYER_EVENT_AMENDMENT)

# AETHERYN CANON AMENDMENT --- GODSLAYER EVENT

## Status

Engine-Level Canon Integration\
Authority: Structural Override Layer\
Scope: Cosmology, Monster Origin, Relic Doctrine, Combat Philosophy

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# I. THE GODSLAYER EVENT (FOUNDATIONAL CANON)

Two mortal twins --- one Warrior, one Mage --- killed a god.

The event caused:

-   Collapse of localized divine architecture
-   Severe Aether lattice destabilization
-   Soul pressure inversion
-   Emergence of monster manifestations
-   Withdrawal and paranoia among remaining gods

This event is historical fact within the world, though interpretations
vary culturally.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# II. THE WARRIOR --- RELIC ASCENSION

The Warrior survived the god-killing.

Due to divine overload, the Warrior crystallized into a Relic-class
entity.

## Relic Classification

Tier IV --- World-Shaping (per Relic Doctrine)

## Fragmentation

The relic fragmented into conceptual anchors representing:

-   Discipline
-   Precision
-   Resolve
-   Endurance

Fragments remain dormant individually.

## Activation Requirements

Full activation requires:

-   One Mage
-   One Warrior
-   Voluntary cooperation
-   Divine pressure spike present
-   Fragment convergence

## Effect

One declared action resolves at maximum allowable narrative margin
within engine constraints.

This does NOT: - Rewrite past events - Modify dice size - Override
canonical locks

It creates a decisive forward canon event.

## Cost

-   Regional instability spike
-   Increased monster manifestation probability
-   Both participants permanently altered
-   Potential amplification of wandering rupture

Tone: The world holds its breath. The act carries sorrow weight.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# III. THE MAGE --- THE WANDERING RUPTURE

The Mage did not survive intact.

The Mage became a migrating metaphysical rupture within the Aether
lattice.

## Rupture Behavior

-   Not fixed to one location
-   Attracted to structural stress
-   Migrates toward:
    -   High arcane residue
    -   Heavy Soul manipulation
    -   Institutional fracture
    -   Divine interference
    -   Elevated Crisis density

## Effects

-   Monster manifestations increase in region
-   Aether instability fluctuates
-   Magic volatility rises temporarily

The rupture never fully stabilizes. It drifts.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# IV. MONSTER ORIGIN CLARIFICATION

Monsters are manifestations of structural stress escaping through the
wandering rupture.

They are not inherently evil entities. They are pressure phenomena.

This aligns with Monster Doctrine without altering mechanical templates.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# V. COMBAT PHILOSOPHY REBALANCE

Magic and Martial mastery are parity-based through asymmetry.

## Mage Strengths

-   Environmental shaping
-   Identity manipulation
-   High-impact volatility

## Mage Risks

-   Strain accumulation
-   Concentration vulnerability
-   Residue exposure
-   Divine attention

## Warrior Strengths

-   Tempo control
-   Close-quarters disruption
-   Resource reliability
-   Tactical lethality

## Warrior Risks

-   No supernatural recovery
-   Precision-dependent survival

Epic duel doctrine: Victory belongs to the side that commits the first
irreversible mistake.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# VI. DIVINE RESPONSE

Remaining gods operate with increased caution.

They fear:

-   Mortal coordination
-   Convergent Mage + Warrior alignment
-   Reassembly of the Godslayer Relic

Divine interference increases rupture movement probability.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# VII. ENGINE INTEGRITY

This amendment:

-   Does not alter discrete resolution mechanics
-   Does not modify dice boundaries
-   Does not override save determinism
-   Does not introduce retroactive canon manipulation

It establishes cosmological context only.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

END OF AMENDMENT

# AETHERYN_WORLD_LORE_TECH.aeth (Section: ECOLOGICAL_CONTINUITY)

# AETHERYN --- ECOLOGICAL CONTINUITY

Aetheryn is a self-regulating ecological system shaped by:

-   Climate bands
-   Migration corridors
-   Residue density
-   Institutional pressure
-   Keystone species balance

Every region contains:

1.  Primary Producers (flora)
2.  Pollinators & Decomposers (insects, fungi)
3.  Herbivores
4.  Mesopredators
5.  Apex Predators
6.  Scavengers
7.  Human Disruption Layer

No species exists without ecological function.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# INSECT & MICROFAUNA LAYER

## Pollinators

### Sunlace Bees (Eldrion / Sapphire)

Primary orchard pollinators. Dependent on hedgerow continuity. Collapse
triggers fruit failure and food pressure.

### Riverthread Moths (Veythar Corridor)

Night pollinators of reed systems. Residue-sensitive. Their absence
precedes fish spawning shifts.

### Glasswing Flies (Southern Sands)

Dawn pollinators of succulents. Short lifecycle tied to early morning
moisture cycles.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Decomposers

### Carrion Beetles (Continental)

Rapid carcass breakdown regulators. Population collapse increases
disease vectors.

### Scar Mites (Shattered Marches)

Residue-tolerant microfauna. Stabilize high-density soil zones.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# MIGRATION CORRIDORS

-   Diagonal River Corridor (north--south seasonal flow)
-   Southern Caravan Loop (succulent bloom cycle)
-   Ironfall Ridge Spine (altitude-driven herd movement)
-   Northern Freeze Arc (cold-cycle short migration)

Residue zones alter but do not randomize migration paths.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# FOOD WEB PRINCIPLE

Producer → Herbivore → Predator → Apex → Scavenger → Decomposer → Soil →
Producer

Removal of any tier forces redistribution across adjacent tiers.
Stability depends on keystone species integrity.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# NARRATIVE INTEGRATION GUIDELINE

Ecology must appear through:

-   Seasonal timing shifts
-   Predator movement patterns
-   Insect presence or absence
-   Carcass persistence
-   Migration timing
-   Crop health variation

Never exposition. Always implication.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# MONSTER INTERACTION CLARIFICATION (ADDENDUM)

Monsters are **not** part of the ecological food web defined in this file.

Ecology governs:
1. Primary Producers (flora)
2. Pollinators & Decomposers
3. Herbivores
4. Mesopredators
5. Apex Predators
6. Scavengers
7. Human Disruption Layer

Monsters govern:
- Crisis-responsive, battle-ready entities that manifest from accumulated structural stress
- Non-ecological entities that may appear, dissipate, or reconfigure
- Encounters whose *mechanics* are locked while *expression* reflects current Crisis

Canonical reference:
→ FILE: AETHERYN_WORLD_LORE_TECH.aeth (Section: MONSTER_DOCTRINE)

Integration guideline (no mechanics):
- Ecology provides *conditions* (seasonal timing, migration shifts, residue storms) that can *contextualize* monster manifestation.
- Monster outcomes can create *aftereffects* that appear ecologically (carcass persistence shifts, scavenger surge, altered animal movement) **without reclassifying monsters as fauna**.

End of Addendum.

# AETHERYN — MONSTER DOCTRINE

## I. SEPARATION LAW

Animals:
- Exist independently of Crisis
- Part of trophic chain
- Reproductive
- Energy-bound

Monsters:
- Crisis-responsive
- Non-ecological entities
- Battle-ready by design
- May appear, dissipate, or reconfigure
- Do not require breeding logic

Animals stabilize.
Monsters destabilize.

---

# II. MONSTER ONTOLOGY

A monster in Aetheryn is:
A combat-capable manifestation of accumulated structural stress.

They are born from:
- Institutional overreach
- Residue imbalance
- Ideological polarization
- War pressure
- Ritual misalignment
- Environmental shock layered with Crisis

---

# III. LOCKED ARCHETYPE TABLE

1. Skirmisher — Mobility pressure
2. Hunter — Isolation punisher
3. Juggernaut — Line breaker
4. Controller — Field denial
5. Executioner — Burst finisher
6. Harrow Swarm — Multi-body pressure
7. Ritual Anchor — Support amplifier
8. Crisis Avatar — Boss embodiment

All archetypes:
- Use 3d6 for combat actions; roll d20 for damage
- No range bands
- Discrete outcomes only
- No scaling with Crisis
- Balanced against player baseline

---

# IV. CRISIS EXPRESSION ENGINE

Crisis always alters **appearance, symbolism, and environmental signature**.

**Core statlines never change.** (HP, DEF/armor value, attack bonus, damage expression, and archetype role remain stable.)

However, at higher Crisis the wandering rupture may attach **universal Expression Tags** that are:
- fixed (no scaling numbers),
- identical across all monsters,
- and treated as environmental rupture behavior rather than “monster leveling.”

Crisis 0–2: Latent Distortion  
Crisis 3–5: Visible Fracture  
Crisis 6–8: Open Instability *(Expression Tags may apply)*  
Crisis 9–10: Mythic Rupture *(Expression Tags almost always apply)*  

## Universal Expression Tag — [Volatile] (High Crisis)

At **Crisis 6+**, monsters commonly manifest as **Ruptured**: they gain **[Volatile]**.

**[Volatile]:** When the monster dies, its body ruptures into unstable residue. The GM expresses this as a short-range hazard (default: **d6** harm/effect in an adjacent zone or immediate radius, using the active engine’s hazard conventions).

This does not change the monster’s core statline; it changes what the rupture does to the scene.

---


# V. BEHAVIORAL INTELLIGENCE LAYER

Monsters prioritize:
- Densest energy clusters
- Visible authority
- Open wounds (physical or institutional)

Each archetype has tactical personality:
- Skirmisher tests perimeter
- Juggernaut fixates on obstacles
- Hunter isolates prey
- Controller shapes terrain
- Executioner waits for vulnerability
- Crisis Avatar forces phase shifts

---

# VI. PLAYER MIRROR SYSTEM

[GM NOTE — Not Player Facing]
! Hidden metric: Dominant Strategy Vector (DSV)
! Tracked over last 5 combats.

! Vectors:
! 1. Burst-Damage
! 2. Control/Zone
! 3. Mobility
! 4. Defensive
! 5. Summon
! 6. Ritual/Magic

! Once stabilized, counter-archetype frequency increases slightly.
! Stats never increase.

---

# VII. MONSTER SCAR SYSTEM

Defeated monsters leave one scar:

1. Residue Scar
2. Institutional Scar
3. Rumor Scar
4. Ecological Scar
5. Territorial Scar

Three matching scars trigger Minor Regional Rewrite.

---

# VIII. LONG-HORIZON MONSTER EVOLUTION MODEL

If a region experiences repeated monster manifestations tied to similar pressure axes:

Stage 1: Recurring Expression Pattern
Stage 2: Stable Archetype Frequency Bias
Stage 3: Emergent Regional Monster Identity

Evolution affects narrative form, not mechanics.

---

# IX. CRISIS AVATAR SUCCESSION TREE

If a Crisis Avatar is defeated while Crisis remains high:

Successor form may manifest reflecting unresolved dominant pressure.
Succession preserves mechanical template but shifts symbolic embodiment.

---

# X. CATASTROPHIC SCAR CASCADE (HIGH CRISIS ONLY)

If:
- Crisis ≥ 8 sustained
- 3+ Regional Rewrites
- 1+ Avatar defeat

Trigger Cascade Event:

Effects:
- Multiple scar types manifest simultaneously
- Trade rerouting
- Faction militarization
- Environmental destabilization

No automatic Crisis increase.
Crisis review required per canon.

## Tag Key (Operator Logic)

- **[Consumable]**: Destroyed on use.
- **[Volatile]**: On a bad mishap, harms the user or nearby environment (details are item-specific).
- **[Crisis]**: Behavior shifts under high Crisis (explicitly described on the item).

---

## Universal Item Discipline (Binding)

1) **No permanent dice changes.** Items never change dice size.
2) **No stacking flat bonuses.** If multiple items could apply, only the single strongest applies.
3) **Narrow leverage only.** If an item shifts odds, it does so in a tight context (one action type) and is always paired with a cost, risk, or limitation.
4) **Facts over vibes.** Inventory entries should be factual tokens (`inv:Item=Qty`, `tag:...`), not prose.

**Margin Shift Notation (when used):** `MS:+1` means “treat the final Margin as +1” in the specific context named. MS never stacks with any other MS.

---

## Standard Gear (Tech)

1. **Sun-Spark Lantern** — [Light 30ft] [Fuel: Oil]  
   Reveals residue smears and fine particulate ash that normal light hides.

2. **Iron-Ration Brick** — [Consumable] [3 Uses]  
   Removes `cond:Hungry` for one day per use. Tastes like regret.

3. **Climbing Pitons** — [Gear]  
   Grants permission to attempt climbs that would otherwise be “no.” If a climb would result in a fall, you may convert it to a hang + `cond:Strained` **once per scene**.

4. **Filter-Mask** — [Gear]  
   Negates inhaled toxins/spores (does not protect against skin contact).

5. **Signal Flare** — [Consumable]  
   Visible for miles. Also visible to things you’d rather not meet.

6. **Healer’s Kit** — [Gear] [5 Uses]  
   Stabilize a dying ally without a roll. Does not restore lost vitality.

7. **Crowbar** — [Gear]  
   **MS:+1** on a single “force-open / pry” action. Loud.

8. **Oil Flask** — [Consumable] [Volatile]  
   Creates a burning surface (GM adjudicates as a hazard zone). Mishap: splash-back.

9. **Whetstone** — [Consumable] [1 Use]  
   The next weapon strike that hits gains **MS:+1**. Cannot be stacked with other MS.

10. **Bedroll** — [Gear]  
   Prevents `cond:Exhausted` from “sleeping rough” in normal weather (does not override extreme cold, injury, or fear conditions).

# AETHERYN CONSOLIDATED AMENDMENT

## Combat Doctrine + Spell Architecture + Relic Doctrine

---

## I. Combat Doctrine Addendum

**Tone:** Fast & Lethal  
- Most combats resolve in **2–4 rounds**.  
- High-margin success is **decisive**.  
- Positioning and timing matter more than inflated HP.  
- No infinite stacking bonuses.  
- No damage-sponge enemies.

### Resource System

**Casters**
- Medium Mana Pool (**8–10 baseline**).
- Spells cost **1–5 mana** depending on tier.
- Hidden **Arcane Strain** builds with rapid casting.
- After several spells in short succession, backlash risk increases.
- Strain resets gradually after combat.

**Martial**
- Stamina Pool (**10–12 baseline**).
- Visible fatigue as stamina lowers.
- Strong zone control and engagement authority.
- Can attempt spell disruption at close range.

**Ranged**
- Lower stamina cost per attack.
- High precision, low control authority.
- Increased vulnerability when engaged in melee.

### Death & Resurrection

- Death is common in combat.
- Resurrection is **Forbidden Knowledge**.
- Requires:
  1. Soul ritual
  2. Relic sacrifice
  3. Institutional sanction
- Return always causes transformation (examples):
  - Weakened
  - Changed
  - Marked by divine notice
  - Environmental echo
- Rare anomalies possible (double presence, bleed-through, delayed instability).
- No resurrection is consequence-free.

---

## II. Spell Architecture Framework (Updated)

### Six Towers (Core Pillars / Player-Facing)

1. **Fire** — combustion, heat, forceful expression  
2. **Water** — flow, phase, pressure, cold/brine  
3. **Air** — breath, sound, storm, static  
4. **Earth** — stone, metal, weight, rust  
5. **Soul** — life/death adjacency, identity, bonds, memory  
6. **Aether** — space, wards, reality-lattice, residue craft  

### Non-Core Domain (Not a Tower)

- **Verdant** is not a tower.  
  Verdant is a **synthesis domain**: growth & living change expressed through multi-pillar interaction  
  (commonly **Earth + Water**, with **Soul pressure** as the identity-binding constraint).  
  Access is rare, political, relic-mediated, or crisis-adjacent.

> **Fate is removed as a castable pillar.** “Coincidence pressure / mythic convergence” is handled by engine doctrine,
> scars, and GM escalation—not spells.

### Spell Structure

Each spell includes:
- Name
- What it does (plain language)
- Resolution method (3d6 for actions; d20 for damage)
- Risk (the balancing hook)

### Spell Lanes

- **Flash (F):** immediate, cinematic effects.
- **Tactical (T):** control, positioning, constraints, or battlefield shaping.

### Tier Progression (Tower Extended to Tier 5)

Baseline tower volume is **50 spells**:
- **Tier 1:** 10 spells (cost 1 mana)
- **Tier 2:** 12 spells (cost 2 mana)
- **Tier 3:** 12 spells (cost 3 mana)
- **Tier 4:** 8 spells (cost 4 mana)
- **Tier 5:** 8 spells (cost 5 mana)

**No spell:**
- Modifies dice size permanently
- Stacks flat numeric bonuses
- Bypasses discrete resolution

## III. Relic Doctrine Addendum

**Core Principle:** Relics are fragments of divine intent. Most have hidden consequences.

### Impact Tiers

**Tier I — Curious Relics**
- Minor effects
- Mostly safe
- Occasionally researched

**Tier II — Potent Relics**
- Strong leverage
- Hidden cost

**Tier III — Dangerous Relics**
- Conflict-shaping
- Major consequences

**Tier IV — World-Shaping Relics**
- Era-defining
- Extremely rare

### Relic Structure (Engine Layer)

Each relic contains:
- Primary Effect (visible)
- Echo Vector (hidden)
- Residual Mark (long-term effect)
- Discovery Path (rumor, clergy, academia, etc.)

**Relics:**
- Never stack numeric bonuses
- Never inflate base dice
- Always leave narrative consequence
