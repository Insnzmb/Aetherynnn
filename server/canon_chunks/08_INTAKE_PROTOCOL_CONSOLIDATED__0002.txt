### 5. Campaign pacing
1 — Slow-burn escalation. Long build phases. Institutional pressure accumulates gradually before visible rupture.

2 — Measured progression. Scenes breathe. Consequences emerge steadily with moderate downtime between crises.

3 — Structured rhythm. Alternation between tension and release. Developments occur every few scenes.

4 — Firm momentum. Escalation begins early. Downtime exists but carries forward motion.

5 — Balanced drive. Each session meaningfully advances pressure. Consequences stack at a reliable pace.

6 — Accelerated cadence. Major shifts occur frequently. Institutions respond quickly.

7 — High tempo. Limited recovery space. Stakes rise sharply within each session.

8 — Relentless forward push. Complications overlap. Recovery windows are narrow and costly.

9 — Crisis-dense pacing. Structural shifts occur rapidly. Few quiet moments.

10 — Continuous escalation. Minimal downtime. Pressure compounds scene-to-scene with little stabilization.
