### 7. Realism severity (1--10)

1 --- Heroic cinematic tone; wounds rarely linger; recovery is rapid;
physics bends toward drama. 2 --- Light consequence; injuries matter
briefly but rarely disable. 3 --- Grounded heroism; logistics
occasionally relevant. 4 --- Resource tracking meaningful; travel time
matters. 5 --- Standard realism baseline; wounds, fatigue, and scarcity
are credible threats. 6 --- Injuries may persist; recovery requires
effort or support. 7 --- Harsh realism; trauma, logistics, and
institutional friction prominent. 8 --- Severe realism; survival
pressure constant; moral complexity heightened. 9 --- Brutal realism;
long-term consequences unavoidable; scarcity defines choices. 10 ---
Unforgiving realism; systemic failure, psychological strain, and
mortality are persistent factors.
