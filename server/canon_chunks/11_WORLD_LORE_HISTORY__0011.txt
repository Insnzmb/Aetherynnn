### NY −4100 to −3600 — The Scavenger Centuries (HIGH)
Settlements last decades at most. Magic-users are alternately worshipped, exiled, or killed.

- **NY −3921 — Lath of the Bone Rain (HIGH)**
  Lath-of-Hands binds an enemy’s dead to march. The ritual decays; the dead wander; river systems are abandoned. The phrase “Borrowing Tomorrow” enters memory.

### NY −3550 — The Oath Collapse (SOLID)
Spoken oaths before witnesses bind reality. Conflicting oaths destabilize it. Wars are fought to force speech. Silence becomes lethal. Written contracts, seals, and registries begin as survival technology.

### NY −3300 to −2400 — Proto-Civilization (HIGH)
Fixed spacing rites, repeated procedure, and early record systems stabilize long-lived camps.

- **NY −3188 — Kharos the Measurer (DISPUTED)**
  Credited with making ritual spacing repeatable. Assassinated (motive varies by region).

- **NY −2614 — Queen Sereth of the Seven Knots (HIGH)**
  Seven rivals are bound by interdependent oaths. Peace lasts 22 years. On Sereth’s death the knots unravel and the land burns.

### NY −2300 to −2000 — The Threshold (HIGH)
Writing stabilizes; trade persists; roles specialize. Magic becomes exceptional rather than ambient.

### NY −2000 to −800 — The Age of Blooded Order (HIGH)
Polities consolidate; enforcement becomes institutional.

- **NY −1987 — The Ash Census (SOLID)**
  Regions begin counting magic use. Obscurity ends. Licensing culture descends from census logic: *what cannot be counted cannot be trusted*.
