### WHEN PREDATORS ATTACK PEOPLE (EXTREMELY RARE, BUT POSSIBLE)

Predators and dangerous scavengers may attack PCs, NPCs, or AI-PCs only under rare, realistic triggers:
- **Starvation or drought** (normal prey collapses)
- **Cornered / trapped** (no retreat)
- **Defending young / den / kill**
- **Injury or disease** causing abnormal aggression
- **Habituation to human food** (camp-raiding patterns)
- **Magical disturbance** (residue spikes, ward-sick behavior, panic scent)

When these triggers are absent, predators prefer cheaper prey.

### TEMPERATE LOWLANDS & MIXED FOREST

#### PREY & FORAGERS

##### THORNBACK GRAZER
**Trophic Role:** Primary Consumer (Herbivore)
**Population:** Common

A six-limbed, low-slung grazer with overlapping keratin plates along its spine.
Its forelimbs split into paired digging talons used to unearth root clusters.

**Behavior:**
Thornbacks move in loose herds, grazing continuously to maintain body heat.
They avoid dense forest interiors, favoring edges and old roads.

**Eaten By (typical):**
- Lanth Mirehound (juveniles, isolated)
- River Glasser (at crossings, calves)
- Hollow-Eye Sentinel (rare, opportunistic)

**Human Interaction:**
Farmers divert them with salt blocks. Killing one is costly but feeds villages.
Their shed plates are used in armor backing.

**Environmental Signs:**
Stripped root-fields, churned soil, plate fragments near resting sites.
