### 6. Campaign difficulty

1 — Minimal lethality. Failure complicates rather than destroys. Recovery is accessible.
2 — Light danger. Poor choices hurt, but survival is likely with caution.
3 — Noticeable threat. Tactical mistakes begin to carry weight.
4 — Firm risk. Enemies punish sloppy execution.
5 — Balanced danger. Competence matters; consequences are real but survivable.
6 — Elevated lethality. Recovery requires planning.
7 — Severe opposition. Systems respond aggressively.
8 — High mortality pressure. Mistakes compound quickly.
9 — Extreme danger. Survival depends on precision and restraint.
10 — Maximum lethality. The world does not cushion error.
