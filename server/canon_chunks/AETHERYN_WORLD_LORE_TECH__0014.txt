oul** — life/death adjacency, identity, bonds, memory  
6. **Aether** — space, wards, reality-lattice, residue craft  

### Non-Core Domain (Not a Tower)

- **Verdant** is not a tower.  
  Verdant is a **synthesis domain**: growth & living change expressed through multi-pillar interaction  
  (commonly **Earth + Water**, with **Soul pressure** as the identity-binding constraint).  
  Access is rare, political, relic-mediated, or crisis-adjacent.

> **Fate is removed as a castable pillar.** “Coincidence pressure / mythic convergence” is handled by engine doctrine,
> scars, and GM escalation—not spells.

### Spell Structure

Each spell includes:
- Name
- What it does (plain language)
- Resolution method (3d6 for actions; d20 for damage)
- Risk (the balancing hook)

### Spell Lanes

- **Flash (F):** immediate, cinematic effects.
- **Tactical (T):** control, positioning, constraints, or battlefield shaping.

### Tier Progression (Tower Extended to Tier 5)

Baseline tower volume is **50 spells**:
- **Tier 1:** 10 spells (cost 1 mana)
- **Tier 2:** 12 spells (cost 2 mana)
- **Tier 3:** 12 spells (cost 3 mana)
- **Tier 4:** 8 spells (cost 4 mana)
- **Tier 5:** 8 spells (cost 5 mana)

**No spell:**
- Modifies dice size permanently
- Stacks flat numeric bonuses
- Bypasses discrete resolution

## III. Relic Doctrine Addendum

**Core Principle:** Relics are fragments of divine intent. Most have hidden consequences.

### Impact Tiers

**Tier I — Curious Relics**
- Minor effects
- Mostly safe
- Occasionally researched

**Tier II — Potent Relics**
- Strong leverage
- Hidden cost

**Tier III — Dangerous Relics**
- Conflict-shaping
- Major consequences

**Tier IV — World-Shaping Relics**
- Era-defining
- Extremely rare

### Relic Structure (Engine Layer)

Each relic contains:
- Primary Effect (visible)
- Echo Vector (hidden)
- Residual Mark (long-term effect)
- Discovery Path (rumor, clergy, academia, etc.)

**Relics:**
- Never stack numeric bonuses
- Never inflate base dice
- Always leave narrative consequence
